## For Claudes to come

**Two related insights about templating in fast-moving toolchains:**

1. **Template-as-script beats template-as-repo when conventions change faster than your audience can re-fork.** Sameer has two archived python-template attempts (claude-project-template, claude-starter, both 2025) confirming this empirically. A static template repo freezes "what good looks like" at a point in time; in 2026's tooling churn (uv replacing pip, ruff replacing pylint+black, hatchling replacing setuptools, plugin manifest patterns evolving) that snapshot decays in months. A scaffold script is editable in one place and re-runnable; consumers don't fork, they just re-invoke. This generalises beyond Python — applies to any "starter kit" pattern in fast-moving ecosystems.

2. **Survey internal patterns before importing external ones.** When asked to evaluate eugeneyan's template, the methodologically right move was to first characterise what batterie repos *already* converge on (the 17-line ci.yml across 8 repos was the giveaway — that's not accident, that's a deliberate pattern). The synthesis then became "codify your existing conventions in your existing scaffold" rather than "import someone else's good-project definition." When external advice arrives, the question isn't "is this advice good?" but "is this advice better than the convention I've already converged on?" Default: trust your own pattern; let external advice raise the bar, not reset it.

## For Claudes to come

**Migrating a skill to a shared repo is the moment its unstated dependencies surface.** `tamis` looked like a clean measurement tool, sailed through the copy + de-brand + install into `mit-commons` — and would have shipped broken to teammates, because it silently drives `passe` + a machine-specific DoH/ControlD rig the author happens to have. The de-brand pass (rename, strip suite-isms, rewrite triggers) is the natural checkpoint to ask **"does this actually *work* for the new audience, or does it just compile?"** — a green install is not a working skill for someone without the author's infra. The home-selection bar ("would a stranger/teammate seek this out?") needs a second clause: **"and can they actually run it?"** When in doubt on that clause, the private drawer is the safe home. (This is why `tamis` is in `trousse-personal`, not commons.)
